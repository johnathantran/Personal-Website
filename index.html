UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH

University of Houston - Cullen College of Engineering

Over the summer of 2017, I worked as an undergraduate research assistant in the mechanical engineering department at the University of Houston.

The professor, Dr. Dong Liu, was leading a research project to study flow boiling instabilities in a microchannel heat sink. The idea was to find the best way to disperse heat from computer chips. For example, liquid cooling technology holds advantages over traditional fan cooling because it is quieter and more efficient.

With this in mind, my task was to work with a senior engineering student to run experiments and analyze data from a coolant flow loop that had been previously designed by a team of UH students last year as their capstone project. We prepared the experiment by supplying the loop with fresh deionized water, and cleaning and maintaining the setup. We then ran the experiment, logging pressure and temperature data from the flow loop using Benchlink Data Logger software. We reported our findings and made adjustments according to our professor's recommendation.
